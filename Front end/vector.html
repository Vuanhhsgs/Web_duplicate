Vector

Common Approach

In problems that require proving collinearity or parallelism and involve special points, we use vector tools as well as the known isotomic conjugates of these points to handle them.

Given a triangle ABC inscribed in the circle (O, R) and tangent to the incircle (I, r), with centroid G and Lemoine point L. Let P satisfy the equation \(3R \overrightarrow{GP} + r \overrightarrow{OI} = \overrightarrow{0}\). Prove that P lies on the line IL.

Problem 2
Easy
Given triangle \(ABC\) with circumcenter \(O\) and Lemoine point \(L\). Let \(P\) be an arbitrary point on the line \(OL\). Let \(DEF\) be the pedal triangle of \(P\) with respect to \(ABC\). Let \(G\) be the centroid of triangle \(DEF\). Prove that the line \(PG\) is parallel to the Euler line of triangle \(ABC\).
